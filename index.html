Alla Kholmatova is a user experience and interaction designer who's particularly interested in design systems, language, and collaborative ways of working.

Job: Head of Design and UX at Bulb, green energy startup in London.
 Past: Lead designer at open education startup FutureLearn in London.

In the last few years Alla spent a huge amount of time working on and researching about her interests. She’s been sharing her insights with people through articles, workshops, projects, and most recently – a book.

Design Systems Book: As the web continues to become more complex, designing static pages has become untenable, so that many of us have started to approach design in a more systematic way. In this book, Alla Kholmatova sets out to identify what makes an effective design system that can empower teams to create great digital products.

#designsystemsworkshop

How can we deal with the web continuing to change rapidly? Approaching design thinking in systems doesn't always makes clear how the core UX concepts—user goals, needs and behaviours, are embodied in design patterns we create. How do we make sure that by thinking about structure and modules we don't lose sight of the system as a whole? How do we strike the right balance between reusability and creative exploration, consistency and brand?

This workshop is for designers, UX practitioners, as well as front-end developers who are seeking to approach their design process in a more systemic way, without losing the essence of what the product is about.

What you’ll learn

  • How to establish a modular system using atomic design
  • How to adapt atomic design to suit your team’s needs
  • How to define and evolve design patterns
  • How to establish a shared design language within your team
  • How to validate and test patterns and modules
  • How to combine the patterns together in a way that doesn’t break the whole
  • How to organize designs and structure code in a modular fashion
  • How to create a living pattern library
  • How to make sure your design system can cope with new requirements
  • How to establish a "modular" thinking culture in mid-size to large teams

You'll also take away a few common stumbling blocks, a few lessons learned and a plethora of practical, hands-on tips on establishing a pattern language within your company. We won’t be touching too much on code; instead, the workshop will focus on design techniques and practical ways of creating a scalable, flexible and modular design system applicable to both small and large web projects.
